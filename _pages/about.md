I am a Ph.D. candidate in Space Physics at **Peking University's School of Earth and Space Sciences** (2022–2028), supervised by Prof. Hui Tian. I am currently a visiting student at the **Leibniz Institute for Astrophysics Potsdam (AIP)** (2024–2026), working with Dr. Julián D. Alvarado-Gómez.

My research focuses on **solar and stellar eruptions** and **star-planet interactions**. I use advanced observational techniques and numerical simulations (including the Space Weather Modeling Framework) to understand coronal mass ejections (CMEs), stellar winds, and their impact on exoplanetary environments.

## Research Interests
- **Solar and Stellar Eruptions**: Magnetic reconnection, flare dynamics, and eruption mechanisms
- **Star-Planet Interaction**: Space weather effects on exoplanetary atmospheres and magnetospheres
- **Observational Astrophysics**: Multi-wavelength EUV imaging and stellar spectropolarimetry

## Key Expertise
- Solar/stellar observations (SDO/AIA, Solar Orbiter/EUI, LAMOST, VTT, HARPSpol)
- Numerical simulations (SWMF, MHD modeling)
- Magnetic field analysis and helicity calculations
- Data analysis and scientific computing

I have authored 10 first-author publications and co-authored 16 additional papers in peer-reviewed journals including *The Astrophysical Journal*, *Astronomy & Astrophysics*, and *Solar Physics*. I regularly present my research at international conferences.
